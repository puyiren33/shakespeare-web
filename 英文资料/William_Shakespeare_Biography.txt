========================================
    WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE - COMPLETE BIOGRAPHY
========================================

Full Name: William Shakespeare
Born: c. April 23, 1564 (baptized April 26, 1564)
Birthplace: Stratford-upon-Avon, Warwickshire, England
Died: April 23, 1616 (aged 52)
Burial: April 25, 1616, Holy Trinity Church, Stratford-upon-Avon
Occupation: Playwright, poet, actor
Spouse: Anne Hathaway (m. 1582-1616)
Children: Susanna Hall, Hamnet Shakespeare, Judith Quiney
Parents: John Shakespeare, Mary Arden
Nickname: "The Bard of Avon", "The Bard"

========================================
EARLY LIFE
========================================

William Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon, Warwickshire,
about 100 miles northwest of London. He was the third of eight children
born to John Shakespeare, a successful glover and leather merchant who
served as alderman and bailiff (mayor), and Mary Arden, the daughter
of a prosperous landowning farmer.

Shakespeare likely attended the King's New School in Stratford, where
he studied Latin grammar, classical literature, and rhetoric. Due to
his father's financial difficulties, he did not attend university.

========================================
MARRIAGE AND FAMILY
========================================

At age 18, Shakespeare married 26-year-old Anne Hathaway on November
28, 1582. The marriage was hurried because Anne was three months
pregnant. They had three children:

  - Susanna (baptized May 26, 1583)
  - Hamnet and Judith (twins, baptized February 2, 1585)

Hamnet, Shakespeare's only son, died at age 11 in 1596. The cause
of his death is unknown.

========================================
THE LOST YEARS (1585-1592)
========================================

Between the birth of the twins and 1592, there are no historical
records of Shakespeare's activities. Scholars call this period
"The Lost Years." Various legends fill this gap:

  - He was forced to flee Stratford after deer poaching
  - He worked as a schoolmaster in the countryside
  - He held horses outside London theaters

None of these stories have reliable evidence.

========================================
LONDON THEATRE CAREER
========================================

By 1592, Shakespeare was established in London as both an actor and
playwright. Robert Greene's attack on him as an "upstart crow" that
year is the first mention of his theatre career.

In 1594, Shakespeare became a founding member and shareholder of the
Lord Chamberlain's Men, which became the leading playing company in
London. After James I's accession in 1603, the company was renamed
the King's Men.

Key achievements:
  - Built the Globe Theatre in 1599 on the south bank of the Thames
  - Acquired the Blackfriars indoor theatre in 1608
  - Wrote approximately two plays per year for 20 years
  - Served as actor, playwright, and business partner

Shakespeare was a successful businessman. He purchased New Place
(second-largest house in Stratford) in 1597, acquired land, and
invested in tithes.

========================================
WORKS
========================================

Shakespeare's canon includes:
  - 38-39 plays
  - 154 sonnets
  - 2 long narrative poems (Venus and Adonis, The Rape of Lucrece)
  - Several other poems

His dramatic works are typically divided into four periods:

PERIOD 1 (1590-1594): Early Comedies & Histories
  - The Comedy of Errors, The Taming of the Shrew
  - Henry VI trilogy, Richard III

PERIOD 2 (1595-1599): Mature Comedies & Histories
  - A Midsummer Night's Dream, The Merchant of Venice
  - Henry IV (Parts 1&2), Henry V, Much Ado About Nothing
  - Romeo and Juliet, Julius Caesar

PERIOD 3 (1600-1608): Great Tragedies
  - Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, Macbeth
  - Antony and Cleopatra, Coriolanus, Timon of Athens

PERIOD 4 (1608-1613): Romances (Tragicomedies)
  - The Tempest, The Winter's Tale, Cymbeline
  - Pericles, The Two Noble Kinsmen (with John Fletcher)

========================================
LATER YEARS AND DEATH
========================================

Shakespeare retired to Stratford around 1610-1613. His last plays
were collaborations with John Fletcher.

He died on April 23, 1616, at age 52. No contemporary source records
the cause of death. Half a century later, a vicar wrote that
Shakespeare "drank too hard" with Ben Jonson and Michael Drayton
and "died of a fever there contracted."

His will, dated March 25, 1616:
  - Left most property to eldest daughter Susanna
  - Left his "second-best bed" to his wife Anne
  - Left 300 pounds to daughter Judith

Burial epitaph:
  "Good friend for Jesus sake forbear,
   To dig the dust enclosed here.
   Bless be the man that spares these stones,
   And curst be he that moves my bones."

========================================
LEGACY
========================================

1623: First Folio published by John Heminges and Henry Condell
  - Contained 36 plays
  - Without it, half the plays would have been lost

Shakespeare's impact:
  - Translated into every major language
  - Most performed playwright in history
  - Invented approximately 1,700 English words
  - Influenced countless writers, artists, filmmakers
  - His plays remain a cornerstone of English literature
